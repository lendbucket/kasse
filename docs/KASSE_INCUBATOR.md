## THE PROGRAM IN ONE SENTENCE

The Kasse Founders Program is a 12-week business accelerator for service business owners that uses Kasse as its operating system — graduates get funded second locations, every participant builds the business habits that make them a Kasse customer for life.

---

## STRATEGIC PURPOSE

This is not a marketing program. It is a distribution engine disguised as a benefit.

**What it does for Kasse:**
- Acquires merchants who are 8x less likely to churn (migration investment + community lock-in)
- Generates 200+ content pieces per cohort that drive organic signups
- Puts Kasse Pay rails on every participant's business from day one
- Creates funded second locations — each one is a new permanent Kasse merchant
- Builds the brand as the definitive voice in service business entrepreneurship
- Creates a referral network (each graduate knows 10 other business owners)

**What it does for participants:**
- Real operational infrastructure (Kasse) running their business during the program
- 12 weeks of structured business education specific to their vertical
- Peer network of 24 operators in the same industry
- Capital access they couldn't get elsewhere (StyleCredit line at graduation)
- Shot at a funded second location (the prize that drives applications)

**What it does for the content flywheel:**
- Application process creates content ("apply now" posts, participant spotlights)
- Cohort journey creates content (weekly behind-the-scenes, wins, challenges)
- Graduation creates content (the check, the celebration, the story)
- The funded second location creates content (from signing lease to opening day)

## THE PRIZE STRUCTURE

### Top Graduate — Funded Second Location

**What they get:**
A fully structured expansion financing package worth $250,000-300,000 in total capital, assembled by Kasse and its partners. Not a gift — a package of financing they couldn't have assembled alone.

**Components:**
1. **SBA 7(a) loan referral:** $150,000-200,000 (Kasse refers to SBA preferred lender — Live Oak, Celtic Bank, Newtek). Graduate qualifies because 12 weeks of Kasse data proves revenue. Kasse earns referral fee (1-2% of loan amount).
2. **Kasse Capital RBF:** $50,000-75,000 (originated by Reyna Title LLC). Revenue-based financing at 1.3x payback over 18-24 months. Secured by Kasse Pay processing. Repayment happens automatically from settlement before funds reach merchant.
3. **Equipment financing:** $30,000-50,000 (Balboa Capital or Beacon Funding). Chairs, stations, equipment as collateral. Kasse facilitates introduction, earns referral.
4. **Landlord TI allowance:** $20,000-50,000 (Kasse helps negotiate tenant improvement allowance from landlord — no capital from Kasse required).
5. **Lone Star Contracting buildout:** Buildout at cost-plus-modest-margin through Robert's contracting entity. Consistent quality, faster execution, additional revenue stream.

**True cost to Kasse:**
- $50,000-75,000 deployed (RBF, returns $65,000-100,000 at 1.3x)
- ~$10,000 program operational costs allocated to this winner
- ~$5,000 legal and closing costs
- **Net cost: $15,000-20,000 for a prize that looks like $250,000+**

**What Kasse gets back:**
- New location permanently on Kasse Pay rails (~$40-80K/month processing)
- Original location more locked in than ever
- Loan on books at 25-35% effective yield
- Content rights to document entire journey (lease signing to grand opening)
- Graduate becomes the most powerful recruiter for next cohort

### Runners-Up (4-6 per cohort) — Kasse Capital Line

**What they get:** Pre-approved StyleCredit line of $100,000-250,000 available for 24 months. Not drawn all at once — drawable in tranches as they hit growth milestones. RBF structure (1.2x payback over 18 months from Kasse Pay settlement). No traditional underwriting — Kasse's 12 weeks of data is the underwriting.

**True cost to Kasse:** Capital deployed (comes back with yield). Net cost: cost of capital (borrowing rate if using LP capital) minus RBF yield spread.

### All Participants — Lifetime Kasse Benefits

Every program participant, regardless of finishing position, gets permanently:
- **Kasse Growth plan free for life** (normally $99/month — saves $1,188/year)
- **Processing rate reduction:** 0.15% off standard Kasse Pay rate for life (permanent loyalty reward)
- **Alumni badge** on their public Kasse booking profile ("Kasse Founders Program Graduate")
- **Priority support** (Growth-tier SLA for life regardless of plan)
- **Alumni network access** (private community, monthly calls, annual in-person event)

**Why this matters for retention:** A merchant who has "Kasse Founders Program Graduate" on their booking profile, a lifetime rate reduction, and relationships with 24 peers on the same platform has a switching cost approaching infinity.

## THE 12-WEEK CURRICULUM

### Program Principles

- **Vertical-specific.** Everything discussed is relevant to their industry. No generic business advice.
- **Data-driven.** Every lesson references their actual Kasse data. "Your rebook rate is 68% — here's how to get it to 80%."
- **Peer-powered.** 40% of value comes from other participants, not from instructors.
- **Action-first.** Every week has one specific thing to implement in their business before next session.
- **Kasse-integrated.** Every action item is done through Kasse. Win-back campaign? They build it in Kasse. Commission structure review? They update it in Kasse. The platform IS the curriculum tool.

### Week-by-Week (Beauty Vertical Example)

**Week 1: Foundation — Knowing Your Numbers**
- Session: Reading your Kasse dashboard like a business owner
- Topics: Revenue per hour (not just total revenue), cost per chair per day, commission math, what your break-even is
- Action item: Set revenue, rebook rate, and new client goals in Kasse for each staff member
- Content piece: "Week 1: [Participant Name] discovers their salon's real profit margin"

**Week 2: Your Client Machine**
- Session: Building a client base that stays and refers
- Topics: Relationship score, what drives lapsed clients, the rebook-at-checkout habit, referral tracking
- Action item: Enable win-back campaign in Kasse for anyone lapsed 45+ days. Enable rebook prompt at checkout.
- Data pull: "Your 15 clients who haven't been back in 60+ days — we'll send them a campaign today"

**Week 3: Your Team as a Revenue Engine**
- Session: Commission structures, goal setting, performance conversations
- Topics: Tiered commission models, retail incentives, the goal dashboard, how to have a performance conversation
- Action item: Update each staff member's commission structure if needed. Set goals in Kasse.
- Guest: Commission stylist who grew from $2K to $8K/month in 18 months

**Week 4: Pricing and Services**
- Session: Are you charging what you're worth?
- Topics: Service menu analysis, price anchoring, when to raise prices, how to communicate price increases to clients
- Action item: Review Kasse revenue-per-service report. Identify one underpriced service. Build a plan to adjust.

**Week 5: The Online Presence Machine**
- Session: Your booking link is your best employee
- Topics: Booking widget on website, Google Reserve, Instagram Book Now, the 24/7 booking advantage
- Action item: Add booking widget to their website. Connect Google Business Profile.
- AI Receptionist setup: Every participant gets AI Receptionist configured during this session (live, with help)

**Week 6: Marketing That Actually Works**
- Session: Campaigns, automation, and content that drives bookings
- Topics: The birthday message, the review request flywheel, SMS campaigns vs email campaigns, what content converts
- Action item: Enable review request automation. Build one SMS campaign. Kasse Color before/after post goes live this week.

**Week 7: Handling the Hard Stuff**
- Session: No-shows, difficult clients, pricing pushback
- Topics: Deposit policies, cancellation policies, how to enforce them without losing good clients
- Action item: Review and update cancellation policy in Kasse settings. Enable deposit for new clients.
- Role play: Difficult client scenarios in small groups

**Week 8: Operations and Efficiency**
- Session: Running a tight ship
- Topics: Inventory management, chemical tracking, supply ordering, daily close procedures
- Action item: Set up inventory alerts in Kasse for top 10 most-used products

**Week 9: Money Management**
- Session: Your salon's finances
- Topics: Reading Kasse P&L, understanding payroll cost as % of revenue, separating business and personal money
- Action item: Connect banking or set up Kasse Banking. Create chart of accounts.
- Guest: Small business accountant specializing in salons

**Week 10: Hiring and Scaling Your Team**
- Session: When and how to add staff
- Topics: The math of adding a chair, hiring for culture, onboarding a new stylist to Kasse
- Action item: If adding staff is in the plan — create a Staff profile for the prospective hire in Kasse to see the commission projections

**Week 11: Planning for Growth**
- Session: What does a second location look like for you?
- Topics: The math of expansion, how to know you're ready, what the funded location prize actually means, the SBA 7(a) basics
- Action item: Build your 12-month revenue projection using Kasse's historical data as the baseline
- Prize preview: Finalists for the funded location prize are announced based on program performance metrics

**Week 12: Graduation**
- Session: Demo day / business presentations
- Each participant does a 5-minute presentation: where I started, what changed, where I'm going
- Prize announcement: Top graduate (funded location), runners-up (capital lines), all participants (lifetime benefits)
- Content: Entire graduation filmed for social media

### Selection Criteria for Prize Winner

The top graduate is selected based on a composite score:

```
PROCESSING GROWTH (30 points)
Processing volume growth from Week 1 to Week 12
→ 30 points for >50% growth, scaling down to 0 points for <10% growth

REBOOK RATE (20 points)
Rebook rate at Week 12 vs Week 1
→ 20 points for >75%, scaling down

MODULE COMPLETION (20 points)
Percentage of modules completed and assessed
→ 20 points for 100%, scaling down

PEER ENGAGEMENT (15 points)
Community participation, peer ratings, collaborative contributions
→ Assessed by program director

PRESENTATION QUALITY (15 points)
Week 12 presentation scored by panel (Robert + external judge)
→ Clarity of progress, quality of future plan, authenticity
```

## APPLICATION PROCESS

### Who Should Apply

**Target applicant:**
- Service business owner (not employee)
- 1-10 employees
- Been in business 1+ years
- Processing at least $5,000/month (enough history to prove viability)
- Wants to grow (not just sustain)
- Willing to commit 3-5 hours/week to the program

**Not a fit:**
- Pre-revenue / hasn't opened yet (different program needed)
- Not the decision-maker of the business
- Processing $0 (no Kasse Pay data to underwrite graduation prize)

### Application Fields

**Business information:**
- Business name, vertical (dropdown), location, years in business
- Number of locations, number of employees
- Monthly revenue range (dropdown): <$5K / $5K-$15K / $15K-$30K / $30K-$60K / $60K+
- Current software (what are you switching from?)
- Current monthly card processing volume
- Do you currently use Kasse? (Y/N — already using gets slight scoring advantage due to existing data)

**Owner information:**
- Full name, email, phone
- Role in business (owner/operator/manager)
- How long have you owned this business?
- Do you own other businesses?

**Written responses (these are the real signal):**
- "Why do you want to join the Kasse Founders Program?" (500 words max)
- "What is the single biggest challenge in your business right now?" (300 words max)
- "Where do you want your business to be in 2 years?" (300 words max)
- "What does a second location mean to you?" (200 words max)

**Optional:**
- Video pitch (60-90 seconds, any format, link to YouTube/Instagram/TikTok)
- Link to business Instagram

**Consent at submission:**
- Content release: "I consent to Kasse creating content about my business journey during and after the program, including photos, videos, and written stories. I understand this content will be used on Kasse's social media channels and marketing materials."
- Processing requirement: "I understand that participation requires using Kasse Pay for payment processing during the program."
- Time commitment: "I commit to attending at least 10 of 12 live sessions and completing module assignments."

### AI Scoring System

When application submitted, Claude API scores it 0-100:

**Input to AI:**
- Full application text
- Processing volume reported
- Business age and size
- Vertical

**Scoring rubric:**
- Processing volume + growth potential (0-30 points): Higher volume = more valuable as a Kasse merchant
- Narrative quality and specificity (0-25 points): Vague answers score low; specific challenges and goals score high
- Growth mindset evidence (0-20 points): Do they talk about wanting to expand, hire, scale — or just survive?
- Second location readiness signals (0-15 points): Do they have the maturity to actually execute expansion?
- Video bonus (0-10 points): Video presence and energy score

**AI output stored on IncubatorApplication:**
- Numeric score (0-100)
- 3-bullet summary of strengths
- 2-bullet summary of concerns
- Recommended decision (accept/waitlist/reject) — advisory only, Robert makes final call

### Review Process

1. AI scores all applications automatically (within minutes of submission)
2. Robert reviews applications sorted by AI score (highest first)
3. For borderline applicants: read full written responses + watch video if provided
4. Decisions made within 2 weeks of deadline
5. Acceptance email (warm, personal-feeling, signed by Robert)
6. Rejection email (graceful, with option to apply next cohort, with specific feedback)
7. Waitlist email (honest about position, encouraged to keep building)

## LEGAL AND COMPLIANCE

### Entities

**Kasse Founders Program** is operated by **Kasse Platform LLC** (or as a program within 36 West Holdings).

**Kasse Capital LLC (Wyoming):** Issues the RBF capital lines and second-location financing. Separate from Kasse SaaS LLC. Clean cap table. Revenue-based financing agreements are not securities — no SEC registration required. (Confirm with attorney before launch.)

**Content Release:** Signed during application process. Participants consent to commercial use of their likeness, business name, and story in Kasse marketing materials.

**No equity taken from participants.** The program is funded by: processing margin from participants' Kasse Pay usage, RBF yield on capital deployed, SBA referral fees, and equipment financing referral fees. No equity, no investment adviser registration required.

**The prize is not a lottery.** Selection is based on merit (performance during program). No purchase necessary to apply. Confirms not subject to lottery/sweepstakes laws.

### Attorney Review Required Before Launch
- RBF agreement template (Reyna Title / Kasse Capital)
- SBA referral fee arrangement (some lenders prohibit referral fees)
- Content release language (especially for minors who appear in content)
- FTC disclosure requirements for sponsored/testimonial content
- Texas state business opportunity regulations (may trigger disclosure requirements if position program as "business opportunity" — avoid that framing)

## PATH TO 500 MERCHANTS / $50M PROCESSING

The incubator gets you to ~200 high-quality, locked-in merchants in 24 months. The remaining 300+ come from:

1. **Content-driven organic signups:** Every cohort's content reaches 10-50M people. Even 0.05% signup conversion = 5,000-25,000 signups. Filter for active processors → 500+ additional merchants.

2. **Referral flywheel:** Each participant refers 2-3 other business owners they know. Referral incentive: processing rate reduction + free month of Kasse. 200 participants × 2 referrals × 50% conversion = 200 additional merchants.

3. **ISO / reseller network:** First ISO hires (Phase 6 of build) — commission reps who get 20-30% of processing margin for the life of referred merchants. 5 ISOs × 20 merchants each = 100 additional merchants.

4. **Franchise system partnerships:** Salon Envy franchisees all run on Kasse. One franchise deal = 5-20 locations instantly.

5. **Competitor acquisitions:** Buy a struggling competitor with 500 active merchants for $1-3M. Migrate to Kasse. Instant scale.

**$50M monthly processing target:**
- 2,000 merchants × $25,000 average monthly volume = $50M/month
- At 100bps net (PayFac registered): $500K/month processing revenue
- At $200/month average SaaS: $400K/month SaaS revenue
- Payroll fees: $75K/month
- Lending interest: $150K/month
- **Total at 2,000 merchants, full stack: ~$1.1M/month = $13.2M ARR**
