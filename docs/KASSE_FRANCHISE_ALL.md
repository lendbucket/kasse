**Auto-royalty collection via Reyna Pay:**

This is the technical innovation that makes Kasse's franchise system superior to any competitor's:

Every franchisee payment processed through Reyna Pay is split automatically at settlement:
- Franchisee receives: (gross payment) × (1 - royalty rate - marketing fund rate - technology fee)
- Franchisor royalty sweep: (gross payment) × royalty rate → deposited to franchisor's Reyna Pay account
- Marketing fund: (gross payment) × marketing fund rate → deposited to marketing fund account
- Technology fee: collected monthly from franchisee subscription

The franchisee never writes a royalty check. The franchisor never chases a payment. The system is mathematically precise and always on time. This alone is worth the franchise creator subscription — existing franchise systems spend enormous effort tracking and collecting royalties.

**Vertical-specific agreement provisions:**

**Salon / Beauty:**
- Color formula standards: franchisee must adopt brand's approved formula library
- Chemical service protocols: standard operating procedures attached as Exhibit
- State cosmetology board compliance: franchisee responsible for all license requirements
- Brand service menu: franchisee may add services only from approved list
- Retail requirements: must carry minimum approved product line

**Restaurant:**
- Menu compliance: 100% of core menu must be offered; approved additions only
- Recipe standards: franchisor provides recipes; franchisee may not modify
- Food safety: all staff must maintain current food handler certification (tracked in Kasse)
- Supplier requirements: approved suppliers for core ingredients (specified in Operations Manual)
- Alcohol program (if applicable): franchisor training + local licensing required
- Health inspection requirement: must maintain A rating; 30-day cure for B; C = default

**Gym / Fitness:**
- Class format standards: approved class formats listed; modifications require approval
- Equipment requirements: must maintain approved equipment list at all times
- Certification requirements: all instructors must hold certifications listed in Operations Manual
- Liability and insurance: minimum $2M general liability, $500K professional liability
- Membership price floors/ceilings: must price within approved range

**Barbershop:**
- Service standard compliance: haircuts executed per brand standards
- Barber license verification: all barbers must maintain active state license (tracked in Kasse)
- Shop aesthetic: fit-out to approved specifications
- Music/atmosphere standards: if brand identity includes specific atmosphere elements

**Auto Service:**
- Technician certifications: ASE certification requirements for specific services
- Parts standards: OEM or approved aftermarket only
- Environmental compliance: waste oil disposal, coolant, refrigerant per EPA standards
- Warranty standards: minimum warranty terms on labor and parts

**Med Spa:**
- Medical director requirement: franchisee must contract with licensed medical director
- Scope of practice compliance: services performed only within franchisee's licensed scope
- Equipment standards: approved devices only (laser, injectable devices, etc.)
- Medical waste disposal: certified medical waste contractor required
- Injectable protocols: physician oversight requirements per state

**Territory conflict detection:**

- Locks the territory from reassignment
- Checks for overlap with existing territories (hard block — cannot overlap)
- Generates territory description for franchise agreement (legal metes and bounds or zip code list)
- Notifies any existing franchisees if adjacent territory is sold (so they're aware, not surprised)

## VERTICAL-SPECIFIC FRANCHISE CONSIDERATIONS

### Salon Franchise

**Unique operational considerations:**
- Stylist poaching is a constant risk. Agreement should include: franchisee cannot solicit franchisor's staff; franchisor cannot solicit franchisee's staff.
- Color formula IP: Formulas developed by the franchisor's brand are brand property. Franchisee stylists use them but do not own them. Formulas developed by franchisee stylists belong to the franchise system.
- Cosmetology board compliance is state-by-state. Franchisor must understand that their standards may need adjustment in different states.
- Booth rental vs. employee model: This affects royalty base. Booth renters pay rent to franchisee — does royalty apply to rental income? Define clearly in agreement.
- The "superstar stylist" problem: If one stylist drives 40% of a location's revenue and leaves, the location collapses. Brand standards should minimize stylist-client dependency.

| Equipment (chairs, stations, bowls, dryers) | $40,000 | $85,000 |

### Restaurant Franchise

**Unique operational considerations:**
- Health department inspections are pass/fail. A franchise that gets a "C" in the news is a brand crisis for all locations. Agreement must include inspection reporting requirements and cure periods.
- Recipe standardization is critical but hard. Consider a recipe management system within Kasse where approved recipes are locked and franchisees access them but cannot modify.
- Food cost percentages vary by location — supply chain access varies. The franchisor must either negotiate system-wide supply contracts or allow for regional sourcing within specifications.
- Liquor license is location-specific and non-transferable. If brand includes alcohol, franchisee must independently obtain their license. FDD must disclose this clearly.
- Delivery platform management (DoorDash, Uber Eats): Kasse can aggregate orders, but each location must have their own delivery platform accounts.

### Gym / Fitness Franchise

**Unique operational considerations:**
- Liability is extremely high in fitness. Franchisee must carry minimum coverage. Franchisor should be named as additional insured on all policies.
- Class format IP: Is the "Signature HIIT" class your intellectual property? If so, protect it in the franchise agreement.
- Equipment leasing vs. buying: Many gym franchisees lease equipment. Agreement should specify approved vendors for leased equipment.
- Membership pricing floors: To protect brand value, minimum membership prices should be set. A franchisee discounting heavily devalues the brand for all locations.
- Kids' programs and liability: If concept includes youth fitness, additional liability and waiver infrastructure required.

## LEGAL INFRASTRUCTURE

### State Franchise Registration Requirements

Thirteen states require franchisors to register their FDD before selling franchises in that state:
California, Hawaii, Illinois, Indiana, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, New York, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Virginia, Washington, Wisconsin

Kasse tracks which states the franchisor is selling into and flags registration requirements:

```
FRANCHISE REGISTRATION ALERT

You have an applicant from California (Maria Rodriguez, San Jose CA).

California is a "registration state" — you must register your FDD with 
the California Department of Financial Protection & Innovation before 
offering or selling a franchise in California.

Registration typically takes 90-120 days.

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:
  1. Consult with your franchise attorney immediately
  2. Do not issue an FDD to California applicants until registered
  3. You may collect an indication of interest but not a fee

[Find a Franchise Attorney with California Experience]
[Learn more about California franchise registration]
```

### Franchise Disclosure Requirements

**FDD delivery methods:**
- Electronic delivery (email with read receipt) — FTC-compliant as of 2020
- Kasse tracks delivery date and starts 21-day counter automatically
- Receipt acknowledgment built into the application flow

**Right of rescission tracking:**
- Some states (California, Maryland, etc.) give franchisees a right to rescind after signing
- Kasse tracks applicable state laws and flags rescission periods
